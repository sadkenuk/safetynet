SafetyNet

Helping communities understand crime in their local area using publicly available police data.

What is SafetyNet?

SafetyNet is a free, independent tool that lets anyone look up street-level crime data for any postcode in England, Wales or Northern Ireland. It pulls together official police records so local residents, community groups and neighbourhood watch organisations can see what is happening near them — clearly and without jargon.

How to use it

Enter your postcode to see all crimes reported within one mile over the last six months. Use the date range to focus on a specific period, and tap any crime type, outcome or street in the sidebar to filter the map. Orange diamonds show stop and search incidents — toggle them on using the button in the Stop & Search section.

Where does the data come from?

All crime data comes from the UK Police open data API (data.police.uk), maintained by the Home Office. This is the same data police forces publish for transparency and public accountability. It is updated monthly and covers all 43 territorial police forces across England and Wales, plus the Police Service of Northern Ireland.

Postcode lookups use postcodes.io, a free and open postcode database.

Your privacy — no personal data, ever

SafetyNet does not collect, store, share or sell any personal data. There are no accounts, no cookies, no tracking and no analytics. The postcode you enter is used only to look up a map location — it is never logged or stored anywhere.

All data processing happens in your browser. Nothing you enter is sent to any SafetyNet server.

Crime locations are anonymised

By law, police must anonymise the location of every crime before publishing it. Incidents are shown at the nearest street, road or public area — never at the precise address. This protects the privacy of victims and is a legal requirement under the Police Reform and Social Responsibility Act 2011.

Stop & Search

Police forces are legally required to record every stop and search under the Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 and the Criminal Justice and Public Order Act 1994. This data is published publicly to ensure police accountability and transparency. It shows the stated reason for the stop, the outcome and basic demographic information — no names or identifying details are ever included.

Limitations to be aware of

  • Data is typically two to three months behind the current date, as forces submit records monthly.
  • Not all crimes are reported to the police — this data only reflects recorded crime.
  • Outcomes are recorded over time — a crime may show "no outcome yet" simply because the investigation is ongoing, not because no action was taken.
  • Stop & search data may not be available for all forces or all time periods.
  • Crime locations are approximate — a pin on a street does not mean the crime happened at any specific property on that street.

Legal

SafetyNet is an independent tool and is not affiliated with, endorsed by or connected to any police force, the Home Office or any government body. It is a community project built to make public data more accessible.

All data is provided under the Open Government Licence v3.0. You are free to use and share the information displayed, provided you acknowledge the source (data.police.uk).

This tool is provided in good faith for informational purposes only. It should not be used as the sole basis for any legal, insurance or property decision.
