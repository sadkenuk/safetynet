SafetyNet

A community tool to help local residents understand crime in their area — independent, free and not affiliated with the police.

Why this was built

SafetyNet was created by a resident of Lyndhurst, a village in the New Forest, after the community was hit by a wave of unsolved burglaries that made national news. The village found itself having to take matters into its own hands — organising residents, sharing information and trying to make sense of what was happening.

The problem was that the publicly available crime data existed, but it was buried in government datasets that most people had no idea how to access or interpret. SafetyNet was built to change that — to put that information in front of ordinary residents in a clear, usable format.

You can read more about what the Lyndhurst community went through in this Guardian article from 2023.

What is SafetyNet?

SafetyNet is a free, independent community tool that lets anyone look up street-level crime data for any postcode in England, Wales or Northern Ireland. It is not a police application and has no connection to any police force, the Home Office or any government body. It simply makes publicly available data easier to understand.

It is built for local residents, community groups, neighbourhood watch organisations and anyone who wants to make more informed decisions about safety in their area.

How to use it

Enter your postcode to see all crimes reported within one mile. Use the date range to focus on a specific period, and tap any crime type, outcome or street in the sidebar to filter the map. Orange diamonds show stop and search incidents — toggle them on using the button in the Stop & Search section.

Where does the data come from?

All crime data is sourced from data.police.uk — a publicly available open data platform maintained by the Home Office. Police forces are legally required to publish this data for transparency and public accountability. It is updated monthly and covers all 43 territorial police forces across England and Wales, plus the Police Service of Northern Ireland.

SafetyNet is simply a lens on this public data. We do not create, modify or add to it in any way.

Postcode lookups use postcodes.io, a free and open postcode database.

Your privacy — no personal data, ever

SafetyNet does not collect, store, share or sell any personal data. There are no accounts and no login. The postcode you enter is used only to look up a map location — it is never logged or stored anywhere.

This site uses Google Analytics to understand how many people visit and which features are used — purely to help improve the tool. No personally identifiable information is collected. You can opt out via your browser's privacy settings or a tracker-blocking extension.

Crime locations are anonymised

By law, police must anonymise the location of every crime before publishing it. Incidents are shown at the nearest street, road or public area — never at the precise address. This protects the privacy of victims and is a legal requirement under the Police Reform and Social Responsibility Act 2011.

Stop & Search

Police forces are legally required to record every stop and search under the Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 and the Criminal Justice and Public Order Act 1994. This data is published publicly to ensure police accountability and transparency. It shows the stated reason for the stop, the outcome and basic demographic information — no names or identifying details are ever included.

Limitations to be aware of

  • Data is typically two to three months behind the current date, as forces submit records monthly.
  • Not all crimes are reported to the police — this data only reflects recorded crime.
  • Outcomes are recorded over time — a crime may show "no outcome yet" simply because the investigation is ongoing, not because no action was taken.
  • Stop & search data may not be available for all forces or all time periods.
  • Crime locations are approximate — a pin on a street does not mean the crime happened at any specific property on that street.

Legal

SafetyNet is an independent community project and is not affiliated with, endorsed by or connected to any police force, the Home Office or any government body. Use of the word "police" anywhere in this tool refers only to the source of the publicly available data — it does not imply any relationship with, or endorsement by, any police force.

All data is published under the Open Government Licence v3.0. You are free to use and share the information displayed, provided you acknowledge the source (data.police.uk).

This tool is provided in good faith for informational purposes only. It should not be used as the sole basis for any legal, insurance or property decision.

Reporting a crime

SafetyNet shows you what has already been reported — it is not connected to the police and cannot be used to report a crime. If you need to report something:

  • Emergency: Call 999 if a crime is in progress or someone is in danger
  • Non-emergency: Call 101 to report a crime that has already happened
  • Online: Most forces accept reports at police.uk
  • Anonymous: Contact Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 — 100% anonymous
  • Fraud & cybercrime: Report to Action Fraud on 0300 123 2040
